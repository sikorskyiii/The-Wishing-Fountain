Some stories don’t begin with words — they begin with a ring

In the heart of Nuremberg’s main square stands the Beautiful Fountain, a 14-meter Gothic masterpiece born in the 14th century. Its carved figures reflect the ideals of faith, wisdom, and the rhythm of life — yet few remember it was never meant to stand here.

Somewhere within the city’s forgotten plans lies the name of the tower that turned it away. Beauty, they say, changed its fate.

Hidden in the fountain’s ornate iron lattice is a small golden ring, almost invisible at first glance.

“Turning the ring three times while making a wish is said to bring good luck.”

No one recalls who first said it — or whether it was ever true — but the legend endures. And like all good stories, it reaches beyond stone and time, leaving traces where few would think to look.

Find where to point your gaze, and the path continues.
