The Wishing Fountain

OSINT challenge · Nuremberg, Germany

Some say the “Beautiful Fountain,” a 14-meter Gothic work from the 14th century, was never meant for the square at all.

Centuries of footsteps blurred the plans, but the trace remains.

Hidden in its iron lattice is a small golden ring; locals whisper that three turns and a wish may tilt the odds.

On quiet evenings, the market keeps its counsel.

Name, place, and purpose still align for those who look.

Everything you need is in plain sight.

Reading the signs is part of the craft.

Between drawings and stone, the answer waits.

Remember: work with originals, not previews.

Under iron and habit, clues survive.

Nuremberg keeps good secrets.

No leaps of faith required.

Each step reveals the next.

Now, follow the obvious.
